Inspiration Boards

One of Israel's best known playwrights, Hanoch Levin, wrote satirical cabarets, domestic comedies and spectacles of doom. I designed these inspiration boards for his doom spectacle “Everyone Wants to Live”, based on Alcestis by Euripides.

Puzna

The main character in the play. He doesn’t want to die and will do anything to prevent his end. He’s self centered, childish, full of himself and has a distorted perception of reality.

Puznabucha and the Puznasmarks

Puzna’s wife and kids. They are vulgar, self centered and grotesque.

Gulgalevich

The angel of death’s first assistant. He treats death as a matter of business and as mere bureaucracy.

1st stage concept

The spotlight follows Puzna around the stage while the rest of the characters remain in the shadows. This reflects the way Puzna sees the world - he’s in the center and everyone else are only shadows to his story.

2nd stage concept

The play deals and discusses the concept of death. Everybody will die eventually and we’re all deteriorating slowly. That is why I chose to resemble the stage to a hole in the ground.

The ball scenes

Puzna convinces himself that he’d been dreaming the entire time, and that his death has been prevented. He throws a big ball to celebrate his life, but the audience knows that this might be his last supper.
